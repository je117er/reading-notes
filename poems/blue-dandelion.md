### Against Nostalgia
*by Ada Limón*

If I had known you were coming, back then,  
when I first thought love could be the thing    
to [save me](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_k0mJYct4UE) after all--if I had known, would I   
have still [glued myself to the back of his motorcycle](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1DPldNasDgY&t=159s)   
while we flew across the starless bridge    
over the East River to where I grew     
my first garden behind the wire fencing,    
in the concrete raised beds lined by ruby   
twillight roses? If I had known it would be you,    
who even then I liked to look at, across a room,    
always listening rigorously, a self-questioning look,   
the way your mouth was always your mouth,   
would I have [climbed back on that bike](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BjI0HIn46l0) again    
and again until even I was [sick with fumes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswOG4nLmvc)   
and the [sticky seat too hot in the early fall](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qW_p_Tj55Eg)?  
If I had known, would I have still made [mistake](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2ckobm58bKE)     
after [mistake](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T8y_RsF4TSw) until I had only the [trunk of me](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gUrfWEmtph0) left,    
stripped and nearly bare of leaves myself?      
If I had known, the truth is, I would have kneeled  
and said, Sooner, come to me sooner.
