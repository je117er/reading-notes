### pepper
normally, in every-day homecooking, 90% of cooking needs can be covered by three types of peppers, differed by shape or freshness:
- tiêu xay sẵn
- tiêu nguyên hạt
- tiêu giã trong cối

it's difficult to say there's the most superior type of pepper. each one is made for a different use case. it would be hard-pressed to see ground peppers in a local mom-and-pop noodle soup eatery in Vietnam, for instance. 

how long does it take for a tiny bottle of pepper powder to be consumed at such a noodle shop? do people buy/grind peppers in bulk, or do they grind the peppers each time they refill the bottles? in her limited experience of eating out and past use of pepper powder usually dated more than a month old, the experience had been lackluster: the food tasted spicy and hot, but there's no aroma to be found.

grounded black peppercorns can lose the aroma quickly if left in the open for a time as well. a 30-minute marinade quickly followed by a fast-to-make dish coupled with you sitting down to enjoy it right away is the surefire recipe to savor all that sweet greatness that peppercorns have to offer.

could it be that noodle shops don't serve peppercorns-in-a-grinder because it's more expensive than pepper powder? that would make sense since aside from a cost's standpoint, notetaker has a hard time seeing any other reason why pepper powders are preferred. after all, grounding fresh peppercorns only requires three to four concentrated motions, each not taking more than one and a half seconds, to have enough powder for a standard bowl of pho. spice grinders and soup dishes are a forgiving match. your dish won't take it as an insult if you can't have even powder, spread evenly on the broth's surface, unless the toppings are sticking their heads out, witnessing the human atrocity of the century the entire time.

peppercorns-in-a-mortar is a curious case. in her history of watching cooking videos (which is not a lot), the only time notetaker'd seen this type of pepper getting used was a cooking reel where the capped man pestled the corns away in a mortar to prepare for an outdoor BBQ. *ah, so peppers can be prepared in that way, too*

to conclude, each type of pepper has its own pluses and minuses; and while notetaker proudly owns two mortars, she can't foresee herself smashing peppercorns in one any time soon.
